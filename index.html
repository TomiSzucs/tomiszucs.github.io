Hi, I'm Tamás Szűcs

I'm a second year Master's student studying Cognitive Psychology at ELTE. I have a deep interest in how humans work. In my research projects and side projects I mainly deal with how we can understand the way we work on a neural level, how we can communicate the way we work and how we can understand things we clearly aren't optimised for understanding (e.g. statistics and science).

Projects

  • Numerical Cognition

    To gain insight into how our minds process numbers, I am examining the relationship of the comparison distance effect and the priming distance effect in numerical cognition.

  • Reliability

    Low reliability is a pervasive problem in cognitive science. I work on the possibilities of raising the reliability of cognitive experimental effects.

  • CogStat

    I take part in the development of CogStat, an open-source statistical tool. It aims at automating statistical analyses and making statistics more intuitive.

Research

I have been doing research in various areas for the past 4 years. I mainly worked on numerical cognition, statistical methodology, the replication crisis and memory processes in the hippocampus.

    HCCCL     2020-

    At the Hippocampal Circuit & Code for Cognition Lab I work on a project in partnership with the Max Planck Institute for Human Development. We are preparing a longitudinal study examining the connection between hippocampal subfield changes and memory component processes such as pattern separation and pattern completion.

    CogStat     2020-

    I am currently working on implementing multiple linear regression into CogStat. Automating more complex analyses like multiple regression and trying to make them more intuitive is very important for numerous scientific disciplines.

    NumberWorks     2018-

    In the NumberWorks lab I work with Attila Krajcsi, the lab head, on numerical cognition and problems we encounter with the reliability of cognitive effects, and ways to try to remedy this surprisingly wide-spread problem.
